This tool helps you estimate your risk of developing cardiovascular disease based on established risk factors. Use the calculator to better understand your health and discuss your results with your healthcare provider.

Risk Factors for Heart Attack

  • Smoking: Damages blood vessels and increases blood pressure, raising heart attack risk.
  • Genetics: Family history of heart disease can increase your risk.
  • Age: Risk increases as you get older, especially after age 45 for men and 55 for women.
  • Alcohol: Excessive drinking can raise blood pressure and cholesterol levels.
  • Unhealthy Food: Diets high in saturated fat, salt, and sugar contribute to heart disease.
  • High Cholesterol: Leads to plaque buildup in arteries, restricting blood flow to the heart.
  • Stress: Chronic stress may damage arteries and worsen other risk factors.
  • Diabetes: High blood sugar damages blood vessels and nerves controlling the heart.
  • Obesity: Increases the risk of high blood pressure, diabetes, and high cholesterol.

The Heart Attack Risk Calculator is designed to help healthcare professionals and individuals estimate the risk of developing cardiovascular disease based on established risk factors. What is a Cardiac Risk Calculator?

A cardiac risk calculator is a screening tool that estimates your risk of future cardiovascular disease. It uses your personal health information to calculate a 10-year and lifelong risk of heart disease. The risk score can help you and your healthcare provider make lifestyle changes or take medicines to prevent heart disease.

What Information Does It Use?

  • Age, sex, and race
  • Blood pressure and use of medications
  • Cholesterol levels and use of statins
  • Diabetes status
  • Family history of heart attacks or heart disease
  • Smoking history

How is Risk Calculated?

Cardiac risk calculators give a heart disease risk score as a percentage. The lower the percentage, the lower your chances of developing heart disease in the next 10 years. The higher the percentage, the greater your chances of significant heart problems now and in the future.

  • Low: Less than a 5% risk
  • Borderline: 5% to 7.4% risk
  • Intermediate: 7.5% to 19.9% risk
  • High: More than a 20% risk

Risk Factors

Things you can't change
  • Age
  • Family history of cardiovascular disease
  • Previous history of cardiovascular disease
  • Sex
Things you can change
  • Blood pressure
  • Blood sugar/diabetes
  • BMI
  • Chronic inflammation
  • Diet
  • Exercise
  • HDL cholesterol
  • Smoking
  • Stress
  • Total cholesterol
Social factors
  • Environment
  • Income
  • Social isolation

How Can I Improve My Heart Disease Risk Score?

  • Eat a heart-healthy diet
  • Get regular aerobic exercise (at least 150 minutes per week)
  • Limit alcohol consumption
  • Lose weight (if needed) and maintain a healthy weight
  • Find healthy ways to manage stress
  • Get help to quit smoking and avoid secondhand smoke
  • Manage conditions like high blood pressure, diabetes, and high cholesterol
